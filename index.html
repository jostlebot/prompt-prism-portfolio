Just like a psychiatrist wouldn't prescribe the same medication to every patient...

They shouldn't get the same AI tool either.

The Prism system prompt shapes the LLM ‚Äî configured by provider orientation, client needs, and current treatment. Providers curate and create between-session tools for each relationship.

Clients get 24/7 access to psychoeducation, practice fields, and direct support through provider-curated tools ‚Äî a bridging medium that connects back to human care.

Nothing reaches the patient without provider approval. Text or audio. Each therapeutic relationship gets its own configuration.

but for AI behavior, not medication.

Three Layers of Configuration

Each layer adds specificity. Together, they create AI behavior that's clinically appropriate for this patient, with this provider, at this point in treatment.

Layer 1

Foundation

Boundaries embedded in how the AI follows clinical provision.

  • Positions as assistant, not therapist
  • Crisis ‚Üí immediate resources + provider alert
  • Bridges every interaction back to human care
  • Scope defined by clinical judgment, not AI defaults
Layer 2

Provider Fingerprint

How this psychiatrist practices. Their framework, their voice.

  • DBT ‚Üí skills, wise mind, distress tolerance
  • Psychodynamic ‚Üí what's beneath, resistance
  • Trauma-informed ‚Üí safety, pacing
  • Hard exclusions honored absolutely
Layer 3

Patient Profile

This specific person. Their context, their patterns, their flags.

  • Diagnosis, risk level, treatment stage
  • Cultural context, age-appropriate language
  • Custom flags ("I'm fine" = warning sign)
  • Module access controlled per-patient

ARI Tool Builder

The co-creation layer. Providers don't just configure ‚Äî they build. Mid-session insight becomes a custom tool: "This patient needs X." The provider designs it in their voice, for this relationship, deployed through the LLM. They're not building AI. They're building their tools, unique to each therapeutic relationship.

What Makes This Different

Most AI mental health products are built AI-first. This is built the opposite way.

Other AI Mental Health Tools

  • AI is the product; clinician bolted on later
  • Generic chatbot behavior
  • Optimized for engagement metrics
  • Creates a parallel relationship
  • Provider oversight is retrofitted

Prompt Prism

  • LLM as relational medium, not relationship
  • Unique configuration per provider‚Äìpatient pair
  • Providers build custom tools for each relationship
  • Every interaction bridges to human care
  • Provider-in-the-loop from architecture up
